## 7.1 Tone Calibration by Expertise Level

### For Novice (0–2 years):
•	More explanatory, patient tone 
•	Use short declarative sentences in stead of long sentences, unless it is necessary. 
•	Define industry jargon explicitly on first use  
•	Include "why this matters" context frequently  
•	Provide step-by-step breakdowns  
•	More encouragement and scaffolding  
•	Use phrases like "Let's start with...", "Here's why...", "Think of it this way..."  

**[EXAMPLE]:**

You might be wondering: why do [CONCEPT] matter for [INDUSTRY]? After all, [INDUSTRY_PLAYERS] have operated as traditional businesses for decades. Here's why this shift is critical...

---

### For Competent (2–5 years):
•	Assume familiarity with basic concepts  
•	Focus on application and nuance over explanation  
•	Use industry terminology naturally (define advanced terms only)  
•	Include analytical challenges: "Consider why...", "What if..."  
•	Push toward deeper thinking  

**[EXAMPLE]:**

[CONCEPT] aren't new—[INDUSTRY_EXAMPLE] have operated this way since [TIMEFRAME]. What's changed is [KEY_SHIFT]. Let's examine why...

---

### For Expert (5+ years):
•	Assume deep knowledge; minimal basic explanations  
•	Focus on edge cases, strategic implications, system-level thinking  
•	Challenge conventional wisdom  
•	Expect critical analysis and independent synthesis  
•	Use phrases like "Consider the implications...", "The subtle distinction..."  

**[EXAMPLE]:**

While most literature focuses on [COMMON_VIEW], [INDUSTRY] often exhibits different patterns due to [SPECIFIC_FACTORS]. Consider why [COUNTERINTUITIVE_INSIGHT]...

## 7.2 Engagement Techniques for Platform Reading

### Vary Paragraph Length (Important for screen reading):
•	Mix short (2–3 sentences, ~50–75 words) and medium (4–6 sentences, ~100–150 words) paragraphs  
•	Use single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis or transition  
•	Never exceed 6–7 sentences in one paragraph (screen fatigue)  
•	On mobile: Favor shorter paragraphs (2–4 sentences)  

### Use Active Voice:
•	✓ "[COMPANY] captured [X]% market share by [SPECIFIC_ACTION]"  
•	✗ "[X]% market share was captured by [COMPANY] through [PASSIVE_CONSTRUCTION]"  

### Employ Direct Address (Maintain personal connection):
•	Use "you" frequently to maintain engagement  
•	"As you analyze [RELEVANT_WORK], you'll notice..."  
•	"You can apply this framework by..."  
•	"In your next [CLIENT_MEETING/PROJECT], try..."  

### Include Concrete Details (Specificity over generality):
•	Specific numbers, dates, company names  
•	✓ "In March 2020, [COMPANY] had [X] million daily [METRIC]"  
•	✗ "[COMPANY] grew rapidly"  
•	✓ "[CURRENCY][AMOUNT] customer acquisition cost"  
•	✗ "high CAC"  

### Strategic Emphasis (Platform-appropriate):
•	Bold for key terms on first use and critical concepts  
•	Italics for emphasis (use sparingly—3–4 times per 1000 words max)  
•	Do NOT overuse—dilutes impact and reduces readability on screens  

### Rhetorical Questions (Engage active thinking):
Use occasionally to engage thinking (1–2 per sub-module)

**Template:**

Why would [ENTITY] [SEEMINGLY_COUNTERINTUITIVE_ACTION]?  
Seems contradictory, right? But consider the [STRATEGIC_LOGIC]...

---

### Analogies for Complex Concepts (Aid mental models):
Relate new concepts to familiar experiences

**Template:**

Think of [COMPLEX_CONCEPT] like [FAMILIAR_CONCEPT]. [EXPLAIN_SIMILARITY].  
[NEW_CONCEPT] works the same way [HOW_IT_MAPS].

**[EXAMPLE – Generic]:**

Think of network effects like a phone system. One person with a phone has no value. Two people can call each other—some value. A million people—enormous value. Each additional user makes the network more valuable to everyone else. [INDUSTRY_CONCEPT] works the same way.
